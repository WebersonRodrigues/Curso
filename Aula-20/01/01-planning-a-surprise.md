# Texto 1 – Planning a Surprise

## 📖 Texto – "Planning a Surprise"

Next Saturday is my mom's birthday, and we're **planning** a big **surprise** for her.

My dad said: "We're **going to** throw a surprise party! But we **need to** keep it a **secret**."

My sister and I got so excited! We started to **organize** everything right away.

First, we made a **guest list**. I said: "We **need to** invite Grandma, Uncle Pedro, Aunt Carla, and Mom's best friends."

Dad said: "Good idea! I'm **going to** call everyone tonight. **Make sure** you don't tell Mom anything!"

Then we talked about **decorations**. My sister said: "I'm **planning to** buy pink and gold balloons. Mom loves those colors."

I said: "And I'm **going to** make a big sign that says 'Happy Birthday, Mom!' We **need to** get colored paper and glue."

Dad looked at a **recipe** for chocolate cake — Mom's favorite. He said: "I'm **going to** bake the cake on Friday night, after Mom goes to sleep."

I asked: "What **ingredients** do we need?" Dad checked the recipe: "We **need to** buy eggs, flour, sugar, butter, and chocolate. **Don't forget** the chocolate!"

We went to the store together to **prepare** everything. We bought all the ingredients, the decorations, and a beautiful **gift** for Mom.

When we got home, I helped **wrap** the gift in shiny purple paper. I'm not very good at wrapping, but Dad helped me.

Dad said: "**Remind** me to hide the cake ingredients in the garage. Mom can't see them!"

On Friday night, Dad baked the cake while Mom watched TV. The kitchen smelled amazing!

My sister **arranged** the flowers in a vase. I blew up all the balloons. We hid everything in the garage.

On Saturday morning, we woke up early. Dad said: "OK, team! I'm **going to** set up the living room. **Make sure** you keep Mom in the bedroom!"

My sister said: "I'm **planning to** bring her breakfast in bed. That will give us time!"

The **guests** started arriving at 10 AM. Everyone was quiet. We were so nervous!

When Mom came downstairs, we all shouted: "SURPRISE! Happy Birthday!"

Mom's face was priceless! She didn't **expect** this at all. She hugged everyone and said: "This is the best birthday ever!"

We **celebrated** all day with cake, music, and dancing. I felt so proud because we **organized** everything ourselves.

Planning a surprise is hard work, but seeing Mom so happy made it totally worth it.

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. "I'm going to..." — Eu vou... (planos futuros)**
> I'm going to bake the cake on Friday night.

Usamos "going to" para falar de planos que já decidimos:
- I'm going to buy balloons. (Eu vou comprar balões.)
- We're going to invite everyone. (Nós vamos convidar todo mundo.)
- She's going to love it! (Ela vai amar!)

---

**2. "We need to..." — Precisamos... (necessidade)**
> We need to buy eggs, flour, and chocolate.

Usamos "need to" para dizer o que é necessário fazer:
- We need to keep it a secret. (Precisamos manter em segredo.)
- I need to finish my homework. (Preciso terminar minha lição.)
- You need to be quiet! (Você precisa ficar quieto!)

---

**3. "Make sure you..." / "Don't forget to..." — Instruções e lembretes**
> Make sure you don't tell Mom anything! / Don't forget the chocolate!

Formas naturais de dar instruções ou lembrar alguém:
- Make sure you lock the door. (Não esqueça de trancar a porta.)
- Don't forget to bring your jacket. (Não esqueça de trazer sua jaqueta.)
- Make sure you arrive on time. (Não deixe de chegar na hora.)

---

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- Nos EUA, festas surpresa são muito populares! A família inteira participa do planejamento e todo mundo guarda o segredo.
- É comum o pai ou a mãe assar o bolo em casa seguindo uma receita, em vez de comprar pronto na padaria.
- "Guest list" é algo que as famílias americanas realmente fazem — uma lista organizada de quem vai ser convidado.
- Embrulhar presentes ("gift wrapping") é uma tradição importante. Nos EUA, muitas lojas oferecem o serviço de embrulhar o presente na hora da compra.
