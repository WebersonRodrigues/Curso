# Texto 1 – My Dream Job

## 📖 Texto – "My Dream Job"

Sometimes I think about the **future**. I think about what I want to be when I grow up.

My dad is an **engineer**. He helps **build** bridges and buildings. I think that is really cool!

My aunt is a **scientist**. She works in a laboratory because she loves to **discover** new things.

I asked my dad: "Why did you become an engineer?" He said: "Because I love to **design** things and solve problems."

My aunt told me: "I became a scientist because I wanted to understand how the world works."

I have a big **dream**. I want to find the perfect **job** for me!

Sometimes I **imagine** being a **pilot**. I love planes, so I think flying would be amazing!

When I grow up, I want to fly above the clouds. I believe pilots have the best view in the world!

But I also think about being a **firefighter**. Firefighters are brave because they **save** people every day.

I think saving people is very important, so maybe that is my **goal**.

My friend Sofia wants to be an **artist**. She loves to draw and paint beautiful things.

She says: "I want to **design** clothes when I grow up, because fashion is my passion!"

My friend Pedro wants to be a **scientist** like my aunt. He wants to **invent** new machines.

He told me: "I want to **discover** something that helps the world!" I think that is a great goal.

Last week, our teacher asked the class: "What is your dream job?"

Many kids had different answers. Some want to be doctors, some want to be teachers.

I stood up and said: "I want to be a pilot because I love planes and I want to travel everywhere!"

But then I thought: "Wait... I also want to **build** things like my dad."

And I also want to **invent** something new, like Pedro.

My mom says: "You don't need to decide now. You are only ten years old!"

She is right. I have time to think about my **future**.

But I know one thing: I want a job that makes me happy and helps other people.

I believe that when you follow your dreams, good things happen.

So I will keep dreaming, keep learning, and keep imagining.

I don't know my dream job yet, but I have big dreams!

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. "When I grow up, I want to be..." — Quando eu crescer, eu quero ser...**
> When I grow up, I want to be a pilot.

Usamos para falar sobre planos para o futuro:
- When I grow up, I want to be a firefighter. (Quando eu crescer, quero ser bombeiro.)
- When I grow up, I want to be an artist. (Quando eu crescer, quero ser artista.)
- When I grow up, I want to build houses. (Quando eu crescer, quero construir casas.)

---

**2. "I think... because..." — Eu acho... porque...**
> I think flying would be amazing because I love planes.

Usamos para dar opinião e explicar o motivo:
- I think science is cool because you discover new things. (Acho ciência legal porque você descobre coisas novas.)
- I think firefighters are brave because they save people. (Acho bombeiros corajosos porque salvam pessoas.)
- I want to be a pilot because I love to travel. (Quero ser piloto porque amo viajar.)

---

**3. "I would like to..." — Eu gostaria de...**
> I would like to fly above the clouds.

Usamos para falar de desejos de forma educada:
- I would like to be an engineer. (Eu gostaria de ser engenheiro.)
- I would like to invent something new. (Eu gostaria de inventar algo novo.)
- I would like to discover new places. (Eu gostaria de descobrir lugares novos.)

---

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- Nos EUA, é muito comum perguntar às crianças "What do you want to be when you grow up?" (O que você quer ser quando crescer?) desde muito cedo. As escolas incentivam as crianças a sonhar grande.
- Muitas escolas americanas têm o "Career Day" (Dia das Profissões), onde pais e profissionais visitam a escola para falar sobre seus trabalhos.
- A palavra "dream" é muito forte na cultura americana. O famoso discurso "I Have a Dream" de Martin Luther King Jr. mostra como sonhar é valorizado nos EUA.
- Nos países de língua inglesa, profissões como firefighter, pilot e scientist são muito admiradas pelas crianças, assim como no Brasil.
