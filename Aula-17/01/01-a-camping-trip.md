# Texto 1 – A Camping Trip

## 📖 Texto – "A Camping Trip"

Last month, my family went on a camping **adventure** for the whole weekend, and it was amazing!

On Friday afternoon, we packed the car with everything we needed: a big **tent**, four **sleeping bags**, a **flashlight** for each person, and a **compass** to help us find our way.

First, we drove for two hours until we arrived at the **campsite** near a beautiful lake.

My dad said: "Let's set up the **tent** before it gets dark!" So we all worked together.

I helped my dad put the poles in the ground, and my mom organized the **sleeping bags** inside.

After that, my little sister and I went to collect **firewood** for the **campfire**.

We found many dry sticks and branches on the ground. My sister was excited because she had never been camping before.

Then, my dad built a **campfire** in the center of our campsite. The flames were warm and orange.

That evening, we decided to **roast** **marshmallows** over the fire. I put mine on a long stick and held it close to the flames.

My first marshmallow got too close and turned completely black! I laughed and tried again.

The second one was perfect — golden and soft on the inside. It was the best thing I had ever tasted!

The next morning, we woke up early and went on a **hike** along a **trail** through the forest.

My dad used the **compass** to make sure we were going in the right direction.

During the hike, we saw incredible **wildlife**. First, we spotted a deer drinking water from a stream.

Then, we heard beautiful birds singing in the trees. My mom took photos of everything.

I was surprised because I had never seen a deer so close before. It looked at us and then ran away quietly.

After the hike, we went back to the campsite and rested. I felt tired but happy.

At night, something scary happened. We heard a loud noise outside the tent!

I grabbed my **flashlight** and pointed it toward the sound. My heart was beating fast.

However, it was just a raccoon looking for food near our bags! We all laughed because we had been so scared.

After the raccoon left, my dad said: "Let's do some **stargazing**!" We lay on the grass and looked up.

The sky was full of stars — more than I had ever seen in the city. I could even see the Milky Way!

My dad pointed at different stars and told me their names. It was like a science class, but much better.

I also noticed many **insects** around us — crickets singing and fireflies glowing in the dark.

On Sunday, we packed everything and drove home. Even though I was tired, I felt so grateful for the experience.

I told my parents: "Can we go camping again next month?" They smiled and said: "Of course!"

I learned that nature is full of surprises, and the best **adventures** happen when you spend time with your family.

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. Sequência temporal — First... Then... After that... Finally...**
> First, we drove for two hours. Then, my dad built a campfire. After that, we roasted marshmallows. Finally, we did stargazing.

Usamos conectores de sequência para organizar a história no tempo:
- First, we set up the tent. (Primeiro, montamos a barraca.)
- Then, we collected firewood. (Então, coletamos lenha.)
- After that, we went on a hike. (Depois disso, fizemos uma caminhada.)

---

**2. Past Perfect — "I had never seen... before" (Eu nunca tinha visto... antes)**
> I was surprised because I had never seen a deer so close before.

Usamos "had + particípio" para falar de algo que aconteceu (ou não) ANTES de outro momento no passado:
- I had never been camping before. (Eu nunca tinha acampado antes.)
- She had never tasted marshmallows before. (Ela nunca tinha provado marshmallows antes.)
- We had never seen so many stars before. (Nunca tínhamos visto tantas estrelas antes.)

---

**3. "However" / "Even though" — Contraste e concessão**
> However, it was just a raccoon looking for food.
> Even though I was tired, I felt so grateful.

Usamos "however" (porém) para apresentar um contraste, e "even though" (mesmo que) para mostrar que algo aconteceu apesar de uma dificuldade:
- I was scared. However, it was nothing dangerous. (Fiquei com medo. Porém, não era nada perigoso.)
- Even though it was cold, we had fun. (Mesmo estando frio, nos divertimos.)
- Even though the hike was long, I enjoyed it. (Mesmo a caminhada sendo longa, eu gostei.)

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- Nos EUA e Canadá, acampar ("camping") é uma tradição familiar muito popular. Famílias vão para parques nacionais e estaduais com barracas, fogueiras e trilhas. É considerado uma atividade educativa e divertida para crianças.
- Assar marshmallows na fogueira é um clássico americano. A versão mais famosa é o "s'more" — marshmallow derretido com chocolate entre dois biscoitos graham crackers.
- "Stargazing" (observar estrelas) é uma atividade comum em acampamentos porque longe da cidade não há poluição luminosa, e o céu fica muito mais bonito e cheio de estrelas.
- Guaxinins (raccoons) são animais muito comuns nos EUA e frequentemente visitam acampamentos à noite procurando comida. Por isso, campistas aprendem a guardar a comida em recipientes especiais.
