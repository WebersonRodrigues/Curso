# Texto 2 – A Video Call with Grandma

## 📖 Texto – "A Video Call with Grandma"

Every Sunday, I make a **video call** to my grandma because she lives **far away** in another city.

I **miss** her so much! I wish I could see her every day, but at least we can talk on the phone.

Today I opened the app and pressed the green button to **connect**. The phone rang three times before she answered.

"Hi, my love!" she said, **waving** at the camera. Her smile always makes me feel better.

However, today the **signal** was not very good. The image started to **freeze**, and I could only see half of her face.

"Grandma, you are freezing!" I said. She laughed because she thought I meant she was cold!

I told her: "No, grandma! The video is **lagging**. Your image is not moving!"

She moved closer to the router, and the connection got better than before.

Then she said: "Wait, I want to show you something!" She turned the camera around to show me her garden.

I could see beautiful flowers and tomatoes growing. She told me that she planted them last month.

"I wish I could visit you and see the garden in person," I said. She smiled and replied: "You will, soon!"

After that, I wanted to show her my new drawings. I tried to **share** my **screen**, but I pressed the wrong button.

Instead, I pressed **mute** by accident! She kept talking, but I couldn't hear anything.

"Grandma! I can't hear you!" I said loudly. Then I realized my mistake and pressed **unmute**.

She was laughing and said: "Technology is funny sometimes, isn't it?"

I finally showed her my drawings by holding them up to the camera. She said they were the best drawings she had ever seen.

Even though I know she says that every time, it still makes me happy because she is always so kind.

We talked for almost an hour. She told me stories about when my dad was a little boy.

At the end, she said: "I miss you so much. **I promise** we will **visit** soon."

I said: "**I miss you too**, grandma. I love you more than anything!"

She blew me a **kiss** through the screen, and I **blew** one back.

Then we said goodbye, and I pressed the red button to **hang up**.

After the call, I felt a little sad because I wanted to hug her. But I also felt happy because video calls make the distance feel shorter.

I think technology is wonderful because it helps families stay close, even when they are far apart.

We **promised** to call again next Sunday. I can't wait!

"Oi, meu amor!" ela disse, **acenando** pra câmera. O sorriso dela sempre me faz sentir melhor.

Em vez disso, apertei **mudo** sem querer! Ela continuou falando, mas eu não ouvia nada.

Aí nos despedimos, e eu apertei o botão vermelho pra **desligar**.

Depois da ligação, fiquei um pouco triste porque queria abraçá-la. Mas também fiquei feliz porque videochamadas fazem a distância parecer menor.

Eu acho que tecnologia é maravilhosa porque ajuda famílias a ficarem perto, mesmo quando estão longe.

Nós **prometemos** ligar de novo no próximo domingo. Mal posso esperar!

### 🔤 Vocabulário essencial do texto

| Inglês | Português | Dica |
|---|---|---|
| video call | videochamada | "video" + "call" (ligação) |
| connect | conectar | estabelecer ligação |
| signal | sinal | qualidade da conexão |
| freeze | travar/congelar | quando a imagem para |
| lag | atraso/travar | quando o áudio ou vídeo atrasa |
| mute | mudo/silenciar | desligar o som |
| unmute | tirar do mudo | ligar o som de volta |
| share screen | compartilhar tela | mostrar sua tela para o outro |
| wave | acenar | mover a mão para cumprimentar |
| miss (someone) | sentir falta/saudade | "I miss you" = sinto sua falta |
| far away | longe | distante |
| visit | visitar | ir ver alguém |
| promise | prometer | dar sua palavra |
| blow a kiss | mandar um beijo | soprar um beijo |
| hang up | desligar (a ligação) | encerrar a chamada |

---

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. "I wish I could..." — Eu queria poder... (desejo)**
> I wish I could see her every day.

Usamos para expressar algo que queremos mas não é possível agora:
- I wish I could visit my grandma today. (Eu queria poder visitar minha avó hoje.)
- I wish I could fly to her city. (Eu queria poder voar pra cidade dela.)
- I wish I could hug her right now. (Eu queria poder abraçá-la agora.)

---

**2. "She told me that..." — Ela me disse que... (reported speech)**
> She told me that she planted the flowers last month.

Usamos para contar o que alguém disse no passado:
- She told me that she misses me. (Ela me disse que sente minha falta.)
- He told me that the signal was bad. (Ele me disse que o sinal estava ruim.)
- My grandma told me that she will visit soon. (Minha avó me disse que vai visitar em breve.)

---

**3. "Even though..., I still..." — Mesmo que..., eu ainda... (concessão)**
> Even though she says that every time, it still makes me happy.

Usamos para mostrar contraste — algo é verdade apesar de outra coisa:
- Even though she lives far away, we talk every week. (Mesmo morando longe, conversamos toda semana.)
- Even though the signal was bad, we had a great call. (Mesmo com o sinal ruim, tivemos uma ótima ligação.)
- Even though I felt sad, I was happy to see her face. (Mesmo triste, fiquei feliz de ver o rosto dela.)

---

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- A palavra "saudade" não tem tradução exata em inglês. Usamos "I miss you" (sinto sua falta), mas não captura o sentimento completo. Brasileiros costumam explicar "saudade" para amigos estrangeiros!
- Nos EUA, videochamadas se tornaram muito comuns, especialmente com avós que moram em outros estados. Apps como FaceTime, Zoom e WhatsApp são os mais usados.
- É muito comum nos EUA que famílias morem em estados diferentes. Avós podem morar a milhares de quilômetros dos netos, então a tecnologia ajuda a manter o vínculo.
- A expressão "blow a kiss" (mandar um beijo soprando) é um gesto carinhoso muito usado em despedidas, tanto pessoalmente quanto em videochamadas.
