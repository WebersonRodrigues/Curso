# Texto 1 – I'm Pretty Sure

## 📖 Texto – "I'm Pretty Sure"

Next week is the school talent show, and everyone is talking about it. My friends and I are sitting in the cafeteria, guessing what's going to happen.

"Are you going to perform, Lucas?" my friend Sofia asks me.

"**I'm definitely** going to sing. I've been practicing every day. **I'm certain** I'm ready!"

Sofia smiles. "That's great! What about Pedro? Is he coming?"

"**I'm not sure** if Pedro will come. He said his family might travel this weekend. **Maybe** he'll be back in time."

My friend Mateus joins us. "Hey, do you think the principal will do something at the show?"

I laugh. "**I doubt it.** Can you imagine Mr. Silva dancing? **It's unlikely.**"

Sofia disagrees. "Actually, **I think so.** Last year he played guitar. **There's a chance** he'll do it again."

"Really? **I'm pretty sure** he only did that because it was the school's anniversary. **I don't think** he'll do it this year."

Mateus asks: "What about the fifth graders? Are they doing a play?"

"**Probably.** They've been rehearsing something in the auditorium. **I'm pretty sure** it's a short comedy."

"**Who knows?** **It might** be a musical. I heard someone singing backstage yesterday," Sofia says.

"**Maybe.** But **I'm sure** about one thing — Ana is going to do magic tricks. She told me herself."

"**Definitely!** She's been showing everyone card tricks all week," Mateus agrees.

Sofia looks at her phone. "Oh! Guess what — the art teacher posted that there will be a surprise guest!"

"A surprise guest? **It's possible** it's that singer from the radio. Remember, she visited our school last year."

"**I doubt it.** She's famous now. **It's unlikely** she'd come to a school show."

"**Who knows?** Anything can happen! **Maybe** it's someone even cooler."

I feel excited. "Well, **I'm certain** about one thing — it's going to be an amazing show. **I'm sure** of it!"

"**Definitely!**" my friends say together.

We can't wait for next week!

### 🗣️ Frases prontas para usar (chunks)

Essas frases aparecem no texto e você pode reutilizá-las para expressar diferentes níveis de certeza:

| Inglês | Português |
|---|---|
| I'm definitely going to… | Com certeza vou… |
| I'm pretty sure… | Tenho quase certeza de que… |
| I'm not sure if… | Não tenho certeza se… |
| I doubt it. | Duvido. |
| There's a chance… | Tem uma chance de… |
| It's unlikely. | É improvável. |
| Who knows? | Quem sabe? |
| It might be… | Pode ser… |

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. Escala de certeza — do mais certo ao menos certo**

> I'm certain → I'm sure → I'm pretty sure → I think so → Maybe → I'm not sure → I doubt it → It's unlikely

Em inglês, existem muitas formas de expressar quanta certeza você tem:

| Nível | Expressão | Exemplo |
|---|---|---|
| 100% | I'm certain / I'm sure / Definitely | I'm certain it will rain. |
| 80% | I'm pretty sure / Probably | I'm pretty sure she's coming. |
| 50% | Maybe / It's possible / There's a chance | Maybe he'll be there. |
| 30% | I'm not sure / I don't think so | I'm not sure if he'll come. |
| 10% | I doubt it / It's unlikely | I doubt it will snow in July. |

---

**2. "Might" + verbo — possibilidade incerta**

> It might be a musical.  
> His family might travel this weekend.

"Might" expressa algo que é possível mas não certo:
- It might rain tomorrow. (Pode chover amanhã.)
- She might come to the party. (Ela pode vir à festa.)
- We might be late. (A gente pode se atrasar.)

Diferença: "will" = certeza / "might" = possibilidade

---

**3. "I don't think" + frase positiva — forma educada de negar**

> I don't think he'll do it this year.

Em inglês, é mais natural dizer "I don't think he will" do que "I think he won't":
- I don't think it will rain. (Acho que não vai chover.) ✓
- I think it won't rain. (gramaticalmente correto, mas menos natural)
- I don't think she's coming. (Acho que ela não vem.)
- I don't think that's a good idea. (Acho que não é uma boa ideia.)

---

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- **Talent shows** são muito populares nas escolas americanas. Alunos cantam, dançam, fazem mágica, contam piadas ou tocam instrumentos. É um evento que toda a escola assiste e torce.
- Nos EUA, é muito comum expressar incerteza de forma educada. Dizer **"I'm not sure"** ou **"maybe"** é considerado mais educado do que dar um "não" direto. Brasileiros tendem a ser mais diretos.
- **"I doubt it"** é uma expressão casual muito usada entre amigos. Não é rude — é apenas uma forma honesta de dizer que você acha algo improvável.
- A palavra **"principal"** em inglês significa "diretor de escola." É um falso cognato parcial — em português, "principal" geralmente significa "main" (principal/mais importante).
