# Texto 1 – Phrasal Verbs Are Everywhere

## 📖 Texto – "Phrasal Verbs Are Everywhere"

Yesterday I was watching my favorite show in English, and a character said: "I can't **figure out** this puzzle!"

I thought: "Why didn't he say 'solve'?" So I asked my English teacher today.

She smiled and said: "Welcome to phrasal verbs! Native speakers use them all the time."

She explained that phrasal verbs are a verb plus a small word like up, out, on, or off — and together they create a new meaning.

I started paying attention, and now I hear them everywhere!

This morning, my mom said: "**Turn off** the TV and **pick up** your toys!"

My dad was leaving for work and said: "I need to **drop off** your sister at school."

At school, my teacher said: "Don't **give up**! You can do it!"

My friend Lucas couldn't answer a question, so he decided to **look up** the answer in the dictionary.

During lunch, my friend Sofia said: "I **came up with** a great idea for our project!"

I told her: "That's awesome! I **look forward to** hearing it!"

After school, I wanted to **hang out** with my friends at the park.

My mom said: "Sure, but we **ran out of** milk, so come home early."

At the park, my friend asked: "Do you **get along with** your little sister?"

I laughed and said: "Most of the time! But sometimes it's hard to **put up with** her singing all day!"

We talked about what we want to be when we **grow up**.

I said: "I want to be a game designer! I need to **work out** how to make my own games."

Then my friend Pedro finally **showed up** — he was late, as always!

I went home feeling happy. Now I understand — phrasal verbs are just how people really talk!

They're not difficult. They're like a treasure hunt — once you start looking, you find them everywhere!

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. O que é um Phrasal Verb? — Verbo + Preposição = Significado Novo**

> turn + off = desligar  
> give + up = desistir  
> figure + out = descobrir

Um phrasal verb é um verbo comum (turn, give, figure) combinado com uma palavrinha (off, up, out, on). Juntos, eles ganham um significado completamente novo!

- **turn** sozinho = virar → **turn off** = desligar
- **give** sozinho = dar → **give up** = desistir
- **look** sozinho = olhar → **look up** = procurar (informação)

---

**2. Phrasal Verbs Separáveis vs. Inseparáveis**

Alguns phrasal verbs podem ser separados pelo objeto:
- **Turn off** the TV. ✓
- **Turn** the TV **off**. ✓ (separado — também correto!)
- **Pick up** your toys. ✓
- **Pick** your toys **up**. ✓

Outros NÃO podem ser separados:
- I **get along with** my sister. ✓
- ~~I get my sister along with.~~ ✗ (errado!)
- I **look forward to** the party. ✓
- ~~I look the party forward to.~~ ✗ (errado!)

**Dica:** Phrasal verbs com 3 palavras (get along with, look forward to, run out of) geralmente NÃO se separam.

---

**3. Phrasal Verbs vs. Palavras Formais — o jeito natural de falar**

Nativos preferem phrasal verbs na conversa do dia a dia:

| Formal (escrita) | Phrasal Verb (fala) | Português |
|---|---|---|
| solve | figure out | resolver/descobrir |
| surrender / quit | give up | desistir |
| arrive | show up | aparecer/chegar |
| tolerate | put up with | aguentar |
| invent | come up with | inventar |
| socialize | hang out | sair com amigos |

Na conversa, use phrasal verbs! Eles soam mais naturais e amigáveis.

---

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- **Phrasal verbs são a alma do inglês falado.** Americanos e britânicos usam dezenas deles todos os dias. Se você quer entender filmes, séries e músicas, precisa conhecê-los!
- **Crianças americanas aprendem phrasal verbs naturalmente** — ouvindo os pais dizerem "pick up your toys", "turn off the lights", "don't give up". É a linguagem do dia a dia em casa.
- **Em textos formais** (redações, e-mails de trabalho), as pessoas às vezes preferem as palavras formais. Mas na conversa, phrasal verbs são muito mais comuns e naturais.
- **Não tente traduzir palavra por palavra!** "Give up" não é "dar para cima". Aprenda cada phrasal verb como uma expressão completa, como se fosse uma palavra só.
