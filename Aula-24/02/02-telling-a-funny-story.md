# Texto 2 – Telling a Funny Story

## 📖 Texto – "Telling a Funny Story"

Okay, sit down because I need to tell you the most **hilarious** thing that ever happened to me. **You won't believe** this. I still laugh every time I think about it.

So last month, we had a big presentation at school. Each student had to present about an animal. I chose dolphins because they're smart and cool. I made a whole PowerPoint with amazing dolphin photos and fun facts. I practiced for days. I was ready!

The day came. I walked to the front of the class with my USB drive. I plugged it into the computer, opened the file, and started talking. "Today I'm going to talk about dolphins," I said, all confident.

I clicked to the first slide and — **guess what?** It wasn't dolphins. It was a baby photo of ME. In a bathtub. With a rubber duck on my head.

**Suddenly**, the whole class went silent. Then someone in the back started giggling. Then another person. Then — **believe it or not** — the ENTIRE class **burst out laughing**. Even my teacher was trying not to smile!

I looked at the screen and my face turned completely red. It was SO **embarrassing**! I had grabbed the wrong USB drive — it was my mom's USB with family photos!

**Meanwhile**, I was clicking through the slides trying to find my dolphin presentation. But every click showed another **awkward** baby photo. Me eating spaghetti with my hands. Me crying at Santa Claus. Me dressed as a banana for Halloween.

**The funniest part** was when a photo of me as a baby appeared wearing sunglasses and a tiny leather jacket. My friend Lucas yelled, "You looked cooler as a baby!" and everyone **cracked up** again.

At first, I wanted to disappear. I wanted the floor to open and swallow me whole. But then — here's the **twist** — I started laughing too! I couldn't help it. The photos were actually really funny.

So I just went with it. I said, "Well, **out of nowhere**, my presentation turned into a comedy show!" The class laughed even harder.

My teacher was wiping tears from her eyes. She said, "That was the best presentation I've ever seen — even without the dolphins!"

The **punchline**? The next day, I finally gave my real dolphin presentation. But everyone said it was boring compared to the baby photos. One kid even said, "Can you bring the banana costume photo back?"

**I'll never forget** that day. It was the most **embarrassing** five minutes of my life, but also the funniest. I learned that sometimes the best thing you can do is laugh at yourself.

And now, every time I do a presentation, my friends ask: "Did you check the USB this time?" Very funny, guys. Very funny.

### 🔤 Vocabulário essencial do texto

| Expression | Function | Example |
|---|---|---|
| hilarious | describes something extremely funny | That video is hilarious! |
| embarrassing | describes something that makes you feel ashamed | It was so embarrassing! |
| awkward | describes an uncomfortable situation | There was an awkward silence. |
| burst out laughing | suddenly start laughing hard | The whole class burst out laughing! |
| crack up | laugh very hard (informal) | That joke made me crack up! |

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. Past Continuous + Past Simple — para criar suspense na história**
> I was clicking through the slides when a baby photo appeared.  
> I was trying to find my presentation when Lucas yelled something.

Usamos Past Continuous (was + -ing) para a ação em andamento e Past Simple para a interrupção:
- I was walking to school when it started raining. (Eu estava andando pra escola quando começou a chover.)
- She was talking when suddenly the phone rang. (Ela estava falando quando de repente o telefone tocou.)
- We were eating when the dog stole the food. (A gente estava comendo quando o cachorro roubou a comida.)

---

**2. Superlatives para exagerar na história — "the most… the funniest… the best…"**
> It was the most embarrassing five minutes of my life.  
> That was the best presentation I've ever seen!

Superlativos dão drama e humor à história:
- It was the funniest thing ever! (Foi a coisa mais engraçada de todas!)
- That was the worst day of my life! (Aquele foi o pior dia da minha vida!)
- She told the most hilarious story! (Ela contou a história mais hilária!)

---

**3. Sequencing words para organizar a narrativa — "Then… But then… Finally…"**
> First, I clicked the slide. Then everyone went silent. But then they started laughing. Finally, I laughed too.

Palavras de sequência ajudam a contar a história em ordem:
- First / At first (Primeiro / No começo)
- Then / After that (Então / Depois disso)
- Meanwhile (Enquanto isso)
- But then (Mas então)
- Finally / In the end (Finalmente / No final)

---

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- **Americanos ADORAM contar histórias engraçadas!** Em festas, jantares e até no trabalho, as pessoas contam "funny stories" para fazer os outros rirem. É uma habilidade social muito valorizada.
- **"Guess what?" é uma das expressões mais usadas por crianças americanas.** Elas usam o tempo todo para começar a contar algo emocionante que aconteceu. É como o nosso "Adivinha?" ou "Sabe o que aconteceu?"
- **Apresentações na escola (presentations) são muito comuns nos EUA.** Desde cedo, crianças americanas aprendem a falar em público. Elas usam PowerPoint, cartazes e até fantasias para apresentar trabalhos.
- **Rir de si mesmo é muito valorizado na cultura americana.** Quando algo constrangedor acontece, a melhor reação é rir junto. Isso mostra que você tem senso de humor e não leva tudo tão a sério. É exatamente o que o personagem faz nesta história!
