# Texto 1 – Helping at Home

## 📖 Texto – "Helping at Home"

It is Saturday morning. I wake up and go to the kitchen.

My mom is there. She says: "Good morning! Today we have a lot of **chores** to do!"

I say: "Oh no… can I watch TV first?"

"No!" she says. "First we clean, then we rest."

Okay… I start with the kitchen. I wash the **dishes** in the **sink**. I use a **sponge** and soap. There are many plates and cups!

Then I **dry** the dishes with a towel and put them away.

Next, my mom asks me to **sweep** the floor. I use the broom and **sweep** all the dirt into a pile.

After that, she **mops** the floor with water. Now the kitchen floor is shiny and clean!

In the living room, I **dust** the shelves and the TV. So much dust! I sneeze two times!

My mom says: "Can you **vacuum** the carpet, please?" I turn on the **vacuum** and clean the carpet. It makes a loud noise!

Then I take the **trash** out. The **garbage** bag is heavy! I carry it to the big bin outside.

After that, I help my mom with the clothes. I **fold** the clean shirts and pants. I put them in neat piles.

My mom **irons** my dad's shirt. She says: "Can you **hang** these clothes in the closet?"

I hang the shirts and jackets on the hangers. Done!

I also **tidy up** my room. I put my toys in the box and my books on the shelf.

Finally, we are done! The house looks great!

"Good job!" my mom says. "You are a great helper!"

"Can we watch a movie now?" I ask.

"Yes!" she says. "Let's make popcorn and watch a movie together!"

I am tired, but I feel proud. Helping at home is hard work, but it feels good!

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. "Can you + verbo?" — Você pode + verbo?**
> Can you vacuum the carpet? / Can you hang these clothes?

Forma educada de pedir algo:
- Can you help me? (Você pode me ajudar?)
- Can you sweep the floor? (Você pode varrer o chão?)

---

**2. "First… then…" — Primeiro… depois…**
> First we clean, then we rest.

Usamos para mostrar a ordem das coisas:
- First I do my homework, then I play. (Primeiro faço a lição, depois brinco.)
- First wash, then dry. (Primeiro lava, depois seca.)

---

**3. "I feel + adjetivo" — Eu me sinto + adjetivo**
> I feel proud. / I am tired but I feel proud.

Expressa como você se sente:
- I feel happy. (Eu me sinto feliz.)
- I feel tired. (Eu me sinto cansado.)

---

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- Nos EUA, crianças geralmente ajudam com tarefas em casa desde cedo. É comum ter uma lista de **chores** na geladeira!
- Muitas famílias americanas dão uma **mesada** (allowance) para as crianças que fazem suas tarefas da semana.
- Nos EUA, é muito comum usar **vacuum cleaner** (aspirador de pó) porque muitas casas têm carpete em vez de piso frio.
- **Take out the trash** é uma das tarefas mais comuns para crianças nos EUA — levar o lixo até a calçada no dia da coleta.
