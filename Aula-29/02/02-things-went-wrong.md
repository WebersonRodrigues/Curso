# Texto 2 – Things Went Wrong

## 📖 Texto – "Things Went Wrong"

Our family trip to New York started with a surprise — and not a good one.

At the airport, we looked at the screen and saw: "Flight 472 — **Delayed** — 3 hours."

Dad walked to the airline counter and asked: "Excuse me, why is our flight **delayed**?"

The woman said: "I'm sorry, sir. There's a storm in New York. We expect the flight to leave at 4 PM."

Mom said: "Well, at least it's not **cancelled**. Let's get something to eat while we wait."

We waited, ate lunch, and finally boarded the plane. Three hours late, but we were on our way!

When we arrived in New York, we went to the **luggage belt**. We waited and waited. Everyone got their bags — except us.

Mom's suitcase never came. It was **lost luggage**.

Dad went to the airline office and said: "One of our bags didn't arrive. I need to file a **claim**."

The man gave him a form and said: "Fill this out. We'll deliver the bag to your hotel within 24 hours."

Mom was upset, but Dad said: "Don't worry. We have **insurance**. They'll **solve** this."

We took a taxi to the hotel. At the **front desk**, Mom said: "Reservation for Silva, please."

The receptionist checked and said: "I'm sorry, ma'am. Your room is not available. We are **overbooked** tonight."

Mom said: "But we booked this room two months ago! I'd like to make a **complaint**."

The manager came and said: "I apologize. We'll upgrade you to a bigger room at no extra cost."

Problem solved! The new room was even better.

The next morning, we went out to explore the city. But after thirty minutes of walking, Dad looked at his phone and said: "I think we're **lost**. The **GPS** isn't working."

I said: "Dad, let's ask someone for help!"

Dad stopped a woman on the street: "**Excuse me**, we're trying to find Central Park. Can you give us **directions**?"

She smiled and said: "Sure! Walk two blocks that way and you'll see a big **landmark** — the Plaza Hotel. The park is right across from it."

"Thank you so much!"

We found Central Park easily. I learned something important that day: **problems happen**, but when you can **communicate**, you can **solve** anything.

Mom got her suitcase back that evening. The airline even offered a small **refund** for the trouble.

Dad smiled and said: "See? Everything worked out. The important thing is that we stayed calm and used our English."

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. "I need to file a claim" — Preciso registrar uma reclamação**
> I need to file a claim. / I'd like to make a complaint.

Duas formas de reclamar educadamente:
- I need to file a claim for my lost luggage. (Preciso registrar uma reclamação pela mala extraviada.)
- I'd like to make a complaint about the room. (Gostaria de fazer uma reclamação sobre o quarto.)
- I'd like to speak to the manager. (Gostaria de falar com o gerente.)

---

**2. "I think we're lost" — Acho que estamos perdidos**
> I think we're lost. The GPS isn't working.

"I think" suaviza a frase, mostrando incerteza:
- I think this is the wrong street. (Acho que essa é a rua errada.)
- I think we missed our stop. (Acho que passamos da nossa parada.)
- I think the restaurant is closed. (Acho que o restaurante está fechado.)

---

**3. "At least it's not…" — Pelo menos não é…**
> At least it's not cancelled.

Expressão para ver o lado positivo de uma situação ruim:
- At least we have insurance. (Pelo menos temos seguro.)
- At least the hotel gave us a bigger room. (Pelo menos o hotel nos deu um quarto maior.)
- At least nobody got hurt. (Pelo menos ninguém se machucou.)

---

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- Nos EUA, se sua mala não chega, você vai ao **baggage claim office** no aeroporto. Eles dão um número de rastreamento e geralmente entregam a mala no hotel em 24-48 horas.
- **Overbooking** é uma prática comum em hotéis e companhias aéreas americanas. Quando acontece, o cliente tem direito a upgrade ou compensação. Sempre peça educadamente para falar com o gerente.
- Americanos são geralmente muito prestativos quando você pede **directions** na rua. Usar "Excuse me" antes de perguntar é essencial para ser educado.
- Ter **travel insurance** (seguro viagem) é muito importante para viagens internacionais. Cobre desde malas perdidas até emergências médicas.
