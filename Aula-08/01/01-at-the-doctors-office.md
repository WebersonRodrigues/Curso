# Texto 1 – At the Doctor's Office

## 📖 Texto – "At the Doctor's Office"

I don't **feel** well today. My head **hurts** and my throat is **sore**.

I **wake up** in the morning and **feel** terrible.  
My mom **checks** my temperature with a thermometer. It says 38 degrees. I have a **fever**.

"You are staying home today," my mom **says**. "I'm taking you to the doctor."

My mom **calls** the clinic and **makes** an appointment.  
She **says** to me: "We have an appointment at ten. Get dressed."

I **get dressed** slowly. I feel tired and my body **aches**.  
My mom **grabs** her bag and we **take** a bus to the clinic.

At the clinic, my mom **checks in** at the front desk.  
The receptionist **gives** her a form to fill out.  
My mom **writes** my name, my age, and my symptoms.

We **sit** in the waiting room. There are other patients there.  
A woman **coughs**. A man **holds** his arm. A little boy **cries**.

After fifteen minutes, the nurse **calls** my name.  
"You can come in now."

I **follow** her to a small room. My mom **comes** with me.  
The nurse **checks** my temperature again.  
She **asks**: "How are you feeling today?"

I **say**: "My head hurts, my throat hurts, and I feel very tired."

She **writes** everything down and **says**: "The doctor will see you in a moment."

The doctor **comes in** and **smiles**.  
"Good morning! What seems to be the problem?"

My mom **explains**: "He woke up with a fever and a sore throat."

The doctor **looks** at my throat with a small light.  
He **listens** to my chest with a stethoscope.  
He **checks** my ears.

"You have a throat infection," he **says**. "Nothing too serious, but you need rest."

He **talks** to my mom: "**Give** him this antibiotic twice a day, after meals. And make sure he **drinks** plenty of water."

I **ask**: "How long will it take to get better?"

He **answers**: "You should feel better in three to five days. If the fever doesn't go away, come back."

My mom **thanks** him and we **leave** the room.

At the pharmacy, my mom **shows** the prescription.  
The pharmacist **gives** her the medicine and **explains** how to take it.

"One pill in the morning and one at night. **Don't skip** any doses."

We **go** home. My mom **gives** me the first pill and **makes** me some tea.  
I **lie down** on the couch and **rest**.

My mom **covers** me with a blanket and **says**: "Sleep a little. You'll feel better soon."

The next day, I **feel** a little better.  
My throat still **hurts**, but the fever is gone.

After three days, I **feel** much better.  
I **finish** all the medicine, just like the doctor **said**.

I **learn** something important: when you feel sick, tell your mom or dad. They know what to do!

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. "My [body part] hurts" — meu/minha [parte do corpo] dói**
> My head hurts. / My throat hurts. / My body aches.

Estrutura para descrever dor. O sujeito é a parte do corpo:
- My back hurts. (Minhas costas doem.)
- My stomach hurts. (Meu estômago dói.)
- My eyes hurt. (Meus olhos doem.) — plural: "hurt" sem "s"

---

**2. "I have a + sintoma" — estou com + sintoma**
> I have a fever. / I have a sore throat.

Em inglês, usamos "have" (ter) para sintomas, não "estar com":
- I have a cold. (Estou resfriado.)
- I have a stomachache. (Estou com dor de estômago.)
- I have a cough. (Estou com tosse.)

---

**3. "You should + verbo" — você deveria + verbo**
> You should feel better in three days.

"Should" é usado para conselhos e recomendações:
- You should drink more water. (Você deveria beber mais água.)
- You should rest. (Você deveria descansar.)
- You shouldn't go to school today. (Você não deveria ir à escola hoje.)

---

**4. "How long will it take to…?" — quanto tempo vai levar para…?**
> How long will it take to get better?

Estrutura para perguntar sobre duração:
- How long will it take to arrive? (Quanto tempo vai levar para chegar?)
- How long will it take to finish? (Quanto tempo vai levar para terminar?)

---

**5. "Make sure + sujeito + verbo" — garanta que…**
> Make sure he drinks plenty of water.

Estrutura para dar instruções com certeza:
- Make sure you eat breakfast. (Garanta que você tome café da manhã.)
- Make sure she takes the medicine. (Garanta que ela tome o remédio.)

---

**6. "I'm taking you to…" — vou te levar a…**
> I'm taking you to the doctor.

Presente contínuo usado para planos imediatos:
- I'm taking you to school. (Vou te levar à escola.)
- I'm taking you home. (Vou te levar para casa.)

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- Nos EUA, consultas médicas geralmente precisam ser **agendadas com antecedência** (appointment). Não é comum ir sem marcar, exceto em emergências (emergency room / ER).
- **"What seems to be the problem?"** é a pergunta clássica que médicos fazem em inglês. É educada e profissional.
- Nos EUA, a **farmácia** (pharmacy) geralmente fica dentro de supermercados ou lojas como CVS e Walgreens. Você precisa da receita (prescription) para comprar antibióticos.
- É muito comum nos EUA que a **mãe ou o pai** acompanhe a criança ao médico e converse diretamente com o doutor sobre o tratamento.
- A expressão **"fill out a form"** (preencher um formulário) é muito comum em clínicas, hospitais e qualquer lugar oficial.
