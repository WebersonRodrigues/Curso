# Texto 2 – Getting Around the City

## 📖 Texto – "Getting Around the City"

Today I **go** with my grandmother to the city. She **needs** to do some things, and I **want** to help.

We **leave** the house together. The street is busy.  
Cars, buses, and motorcycles **pass** by. I **hold** my grandmother's hand.

First, we need to find the **pharmacy**.  
My grandmother **stops** and **asks** a woman on the sidewalk:  
"**Excuse me**, do you know where the pharmacy is?"

The woman **smiles** and **says**:  
"Sure! **Go straight** for two blocks, then **turn right**. It's **next to** the bakery."

"Thank you!"

We **walk** two blocks and **turn right**.  
I **see** the bakery — and right **next to** it, the pharmacy. I found it!

My grandmother **goes inside** and **buys** her medicine. I **wait** near the door.

Now we need the **post office**. But where is it?

My grandmother **asks** a man at the bus stop:  
"**Excuse me**, **how do I get to** the post office?"

He **says**: "It's a bit far. You should **take** the bus. The number 42 **stops** right in front of it."

"**How far** is it?"

"About ten minutes by bus."

We **wait** for the bus. It **arrives** in five minutes.  
We **get on** and **pay** the fare. I **sit** near the window.

I **watch** the city through the window.  
I **see** a **gas station**, a **hospital**, a **school**, and a big **park** with trees.  
I **point** and **say**: "Look, grandma! That park is huge!"

The bus **stops**. We **get off** in front of the post office.

Inside, my grandmother **sends** a package to my uncle in another city.  
The clerk **asks**: "Where is this going?"  
My grandmother **gives** the address and **pays** for the shipping.

Done! Now the last stop: the **library**.

My grandmother **asks** a security guard: "**Excuse me**, is the library **near here**?"

He **points** down the street and **says**:  
"Yes! **Go straight** and **turn left** at the traffic light. It's **between** the church and the fire station. You can't miss it."

We **follow** his directions.  
We **walk** past a **restaurant**, a **hair salon**, and a **pet shop**.  
I **look** at the animals in the pet shop window. So cute!

At the **traffic light**, we **turn left**.

And there it is — the library, right **between** the church and the fire station.

We **go inside**. The library is quiet and cool.  
My grandmother **finds** a book for her. I **choose** a book with pictures.  
We **sit** at a table and **read** together.

On our way home, we **walk** slowly.  
We **pass** by a **flower shop**, a **laundromat**, and a **parking lot**.

I **feel** happy. I **helped** my grandmother today.

She **smiles** and **says**: "Thank you for coming with me. You are a great helper!"

I **learned** something today: when you don't know where to go, just **ask**.  
People are usually happy to help.

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. "How do I get to…?" — como eu chego a…?**
> How do I get to the post office?

Forma mais comum de pedir direções em inglês:
- How do I get to the airport? (Como eu chego ao aeroporto?)
- How do I get to the train station? (Como eu chego à estação de trem?)
- How do I get to your house? (Como eu chego na sua casa?)

---

**2. Preposições de lugar — next to, between, near**
> It's next to the bakery. / It's between the church and the fire station.

Preposições essenciais para localização:
- **next to** = ao lado de
- **between** = entre (dois lugares)
- **near** = perto de
- **in front of** = na frente de
- **behind** = atrás de

---

**3. Imperativos para direções — Go, Turn, Take**
> Go straight. / Turn right. / Take the bus.

Comandos diretos para dar instruções:
- **Go straight** = siga em frente
- **Turn right/left** = vire à direita/esquerda
- **Take the bus** = pegue o ônibus
- **Walk past** = passe por (andando)

---

**4. "How far is it?" — qual a distância?**
> How far is it? — About ten minutes by bus.

Pergunta sobre distância. Respostas comuns:
- It's five minutes on foot. (São cinco minutos a pé.)
- It's two blocks from here. (São dois quarteirões daqui.)
- It's about one kilometer. (É cerca de um quilômetro.)

---

**5. "Get on" / "Get off" — embarcar / desembarcar**
> We get on the bus. / We get off in front of the post office.

Phrasal verbs para transporte público:
- **get on** = embarcar (ônibus, trem, avião)
- **get off** = desembarcar / descer
- **get in** = entrar (carro, táxi)
- **get out of** = sair (carro, táxi)

---

**6. "You can't miss it" — não tem como errar**
> It's between the church and the fire station. You can't miss it.

Expressão muito usada ao dar direções. Significa que o lugar é fácil de encontrar:
- The school is on the corner. You can't miss it. (A escola fica na esquina. Não tem como errar.)

---

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- Nos EUA, as cidades são organizadas em **blocks** (quarteirões). Direções são frequentemente dadas em blocos: "Go two blocks and turn left."
- A **library** (biblioteca) é um lugar muito importante nas cidades americanas. Além de livros, oferece internet grátis, eventos comunitários e espaço de estudo. Crianças adoram ir!
- **"Excuse me"** é a forma educada de abordar um estranho na rua. Nunca comece uma pergunta sem dizer isso primeiro.
- O **post office** (correio) nos EUA é operado pelo governo (USPS). É onde você envia cartas e pacotes.
- Nos EUA, é muito comum crianças acompanharem avós em tarefas do dia a dia. É uma forma de aprender sobre a cidade e passar tempo juntos.
