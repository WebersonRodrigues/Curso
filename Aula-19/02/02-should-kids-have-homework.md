# Texto 2 – Should Kids Have Homework?

## 📖 Texto – "Should Kids Have Homework?"

Last week, our teacher organized a **debate** in class. The topic was: "Should kids have homework?" Everyone had a strong **opinion** about it!

Our teacher explained the rules: "You need to present **arguments** for both sides. Think about the **advantages** and **disadvantages** before giving your **point of view**."

I was nervous because I had never done a **debate** before, but I wanted to share my **opinion** with the class.

First, I presented the **advantages** of homework. **On one hand**, homework helps us practice what we learned in class.

When we do exercises at home, we remember things better. It also teaches us **responsibility** because we have to manage our time.

**Some people think** that homework makes students get better grades because they study more. My friend Julia said: "**I agree** with that because my grades improved when I started doing all my homework."

Another **argument** in favor is that homework helps parents see what their children are learning at school.

**On the other hand**, there are also **disadvantages**. Kids need time to **relax** and play after school.

Too much homework can create **pressure** and make children feel tired and stressed. That is not **fair** to kids who already spend many hours at school.

**Some people think** homework is good. **However, others believe** that children need a **balance** between studying and having fun.

My friend Mateus said: "**I disagree** with homework every day because I cannot **focus** when I am too tired. I need time to rest."

**From my point of view**, he has a good **point of view**. If we are exhausted, we cannot learn well.

Another **disadvantage** is that some kids do not have help at home. That is **unfair** because not everyone has the same conditions.

Then our teacher asked: "So what is the solution?" Many students raised their hands.

Sofia said: "**In my opinion**, teachers should give less homework but make it more interesting. Quality is better than quantity."

Lucas said: "**I agree** with Sofia **because** boring homework does not help anyone **focus** or learn."

I thought about both sides carefully. There were good **arguments** on each side of the **debate**.

Finally, it was my turn to speak. I said: "**In my opinion**, homework is important, but there should be a **balance**."

I explained: "**On one hand**, we need practice to learn. **On the other hand**, we also need time to **relax** and be kids."

I continued: "I think the best solution is to have homework three times a week, not every day. This way, we have **responsibility** but also free time."

My teacher smiled and said: "That is a very mature and **balanced** **opinion**! You considered both sides before making your decision."

She told us: "In a good **debate**, you do not just say what you think. You listen to others, consider different **arguments**, and then form your **opinion**."

I learned that it is okay to **agree** with some things and **disagree** with others. The important thing is to explain why.

I also learned that **fair** solutions usually come from listening to everyone's **point of view** and finding a **balance**.

The **debate** was really fun! Now I want to have more discussions like this because they help me think clearly and **focus** on what really matters.

From now on, when someone asks my **opinion**, I will always try to see both sides before I answer!

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. Apresentar dois lados — "On one hand... On the other hand..." (Por um lado... Por outro lado...)**
> On one hand, homework helps us practice. On the other hand, we need time to relax.

Usamos essa estrutura para mostrar dois lados de um assunto de forma equilibrada. É muito usada em debates e redações:
- On one hand, video games are fun. On the other hand, they can take too much time. (Por um lado, videogames são divertidos. Por outro lado, podem tomar tempo demais.)
- On one hand, pets are great company. On the other hand, they need a lot of care. (Por um lado, animais de estimação são ótima companhia. Por outro lado, precisam de muito cuidado.)
- On one hand, social media connects people. On the other hand, it can be distracting. (Por um lado, redes sociais conectam pessoas. Por outro lado, podem distrair.)

---

**2. Contrastar opiniões — "Some people think... However, others believe..." (Algumas pessoas acham... No entanto, outros acreditam...)**
> Some people think homework is good. However, others believe children need a balance.

Usamos essa estrutura para apresentar opiniões diferentes de forma respeitosa. "However" (no entanto) conecta as duas ideias opostas:
- Some people think uniforms are good. However, others believe kids should choose their clothes. (Algumas pessoas acham que uniformes são bons. No entanto, outros acreditam que crianças deveriam escolher suas roupas.)
- Some people think technology helps learning. However, others believe it is a distraction. (Algumas pessoas acham que tecnologia ajuda no aprendizado. No entanto, outros acreditam que é uma distração.)
- Some people think sports are the most important activity. However, others believe reading is more valuable. (Algumas pessoas acham que esportes são a atividade mais importante. No entanto, outros acreditam que leitura é mais valiosa.)

---

**3. Dar opinião com justificativa — "In my opinion... because..." (Na minha opinião... porque...)**
> In my opinion, homework is important, but there should be a balance.

Usamos "In my opinion" para deixar claro que estamos dando NOSSA opinião, não um fato. Sempre adicionamos "because" para explicar o motivo:
- In my opinion, recess should be longer because kids need to move and play. (Na minha opinião, o recreio deveria ser mais longo porque crianças precisam se mexer e brincar.)
- In my opinion, reading is important because it helps you learn new words. (Na minha opinião, ler é importante porque ajuda a aprender palavras novas.)
- In my opinion, teamwork is better than working alone because you learn from others. (Na minha opinião, trabalho em equipe é melhor que trabalhar sozinho porque você aprende com os outros.)

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- Debates em sala de aula são muito comuns em escolas de países anglófonos. Desde cedo, as crianças aprendem a apresentar argumentos, ouvir o outro lado e formar opiniões fundamentadas. Isso é considerado uma habilidade essencial para a vida adulta e para a cidadania.
- A questão do dever de casa ("homework debate") é um tema real e muito discutido em países como Estados Unidos, Reino Unido e Austrália. Alguns países, como a Finlândia, dão muito pouco dever de casa e mesmo assim têm excelentes resultados educacionais.
- Em inglês, expressões como "On one hand... On the other hand..." e "In my opinion..." são ensinadas desde cedo como ferramentas de argumentação. Saber apresentar os dois lados de um assunto antes de dar sua opinião é considerado sinal de maturidade intelectual.
- O conceito de "balance" (equilíbrio) é muito valorizado na cultura anglófona, especialmente o "work-life balance" (equilíbrio entre trabalho e vida pessoal). Ensinar crianças a buscar equilíbrio entre estudo e diversão é visto como fundamental para o bem-estar emocional.
