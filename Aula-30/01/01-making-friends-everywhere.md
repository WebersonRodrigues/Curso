# Texto 1 – Making Friends Everywhere

## 📖 Texto – "Making Friends Everywhere"

It's our third day in Miami, and the sun is shining. I'm at the hotel pool with Mom and Dad.

I jump in the water and start swimming. A boy about my age is playing with a ball nearby.

He throws the ball and it lands near me. I grab it and throw it back.

He smiles and says: "Thanks! I'm Oliver. **Where are you from**?"

I say: "I'm Lucas, from Brazil! **Where are you from**?"

"I'm from London, England. We're here on holiday too!"

I ask: "**How long** are you staying?"

Oliver says: "One more week. What about you?"

"We leave on Saturday. That's four more days!"

We start playing together in the pool. Oliver is really fun!

His mom walks over to my mom and says: "Hi! I'm Sarah. Your son and my son seem to be getting along well!"

Mom smiles and says: "I'm Carla. **Pleasure meeting you**! Are you enjoying Miami?"

Sarah says: "Oh, we love it! Do you have any **recommendations**? We're looking for good **local** restaurants."

Mom says: "There's a great seafood place two blocks from here. I totally **recommend** it!"

Sarah says: "That sounds wonderful! Would you like to **join us** for dinner tonight?"

Mom looks at Dad. Dad smiles and says: "We'd love to! That sounds great."

Oliver and I high-five. "Yes! Dinner together!"

At dinner, our families talk and laugh like old friends. Oliver's dad, James, tells us about **tourist** spots we should visit.

"You should go to the Everglades," he says. "It's amazing!"

Dad says: "Thanks for the tip! We'll definitely go."

After dinner, Oliver asks me: "Do you have Instagram? Let's **exchange contacts** so we can **keep in touch**!"

I say: "I don't have Instagram yet, but my mom does! And I have my dad's WhatsApp."

Our parents **exchange** phone numbers and **social media**. Sarah says: "Let's **stay in touch**! Maybe we can visit Brazil someday."

Mom says: "You're always welcome! It was a **pleasure meeting** your family."

Oliver and I shake hands and he says: "See you online, Lucas! Maybe we can play games together."

I smile and say: "Definitely! **It was great hanging out** with you!"

Walking back to the hotel, I feel happy. I made a friend from another country — and it all started with a ball in the pool.

Dad puts his hand on my shoulder and says: "See? When you try, **making friends everywhere** is easy."

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. "Where are you from?" — De onde você é?**
> I'm from Brazil. / I'm from London, England.

Pergunta essencial para iniciar conversa com estrangeiros:
- Where are you from? — I'm from São Paulo. (De onde você é? — Sou de São Paulo.)
- Where in Brazil are you from? (De que parte do Brasil você é?)
- We're from the south of England. (Somos do sul da Inglaterra.)

---

**2. "Would you like to…?" — Gostaria de…?**
> Would you like to join us for dinner tonight?

Forma educada de convidar alguém:
- Would you like to come with us? (Gostaria de vir com a gente?)
- Would you like to sit here? (Gostaria de sentar aqui?)
- Would you like some water? (Gostaria de um pouco de água?)

---

**3. "Let's + verbo" — Vamos + verbo**
> Let's exchange contacts! / Let's keep in touch!

Forma de sugerir fazer algo juntos:
- Let's go to the beach! (Vamos à praia!)
- Let's meet tomorrow. (Vamos nos encontrar amanhã.)
- Let's take a photo together! (Vamos tirar uma foto juntos!)

---

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- Em países de língua inglesa, é muito comum iniciar conversas com estranhos em lugares como piscinas, parques e filas. Perguntar "Where are you from?" é a forma mais natural de começar.
- **"Pleasure meeting you"** é uma frase de despedida educada que mostra que você gostou de conhecer a pessoa. Pode ser usada ao se despedir de alguém que acabou de conhecer.
- Trocar **redes sociais** (social media) é a forma moderna de manter contato com pessoas que você conhece em viagens. Instagram e WhatsApp são os mais comuns internacionalmente.
- Ingleses usam "holiday" em vez de "vacation" para férias. Ambas as formas estão corretas — é apenas uma diferença entre inglês britânico e americano.
