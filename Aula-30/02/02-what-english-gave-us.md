# Texto 2 – What English Gave Us

## 📖 Texto – "What English Gave Us"

It's Sunday evening. The trip is over, and we're back home.

I'm sitting on the couch with Dad. The house is quiet. We're both tired but happy.

Dad looks at me and says: "Lucas, can you believe how far we've come on this **journey**?"

I smile and say: "Dad, remember when I couldn't understand anything in English? Not even a simple song!"

He laughs and says: "And remember when I was scared to talk to the hotel receptionist? I used to practice the sentence ten times before saying it!"

I say: "Now I can understand my favorite songs. I know what they're really saying!"

Dad says: "And now I can travel with **confidence**. I can ask for help, order food, and even make **complaints** when something goes wrong."

I think for a moment and say: "I made a friend from England, Dad. A real friend! We talk online every week now."

Dad smiles: "That's your **achievement**, Lucas. You did that by yourself. I'm so **proud** of you."

I feel warm inside. "I'm **proud** of you too, Dad. Remember when that tourist was lost downtown and you helped him with **directions**? His face was so happy!"

Dad says: "That was a great moment. I felt useful. English gave me that — the ability to **connect** with people from anywhere."

I say: "We're not **fluent** yet, right?"

Dad shakes his head: "No, not yet. But that's okay. We can **communicate**. We can understand and be understood. That's what matters."

I nod and say: "And we can always **improve**. Every day, a little more."

Dad says: "Exactly. Learning English is not a destination — it's a **journey**. And we've already reached so many **milestones**."

I count on my fingers: "We can introduce ourselves, order food, ask for directions, travel, make friends, solve problems…"

Dad adds: "Read articles, watch movies without subtitles sometimes, help tourists, and even think in English!"

I laugh: "That's true! Sometimes I think in English without noticing!"

Dad puts his arm around me and says: "You know what I'm most **grateful** for? That we did this together. Father and son, learning side by side."

I lean on his shoulder and say: "Me too, Dad. This was the best **opportunity** we ever gave ourselves."

Dad says: "And this isn't the end. It's just the **beginning**. The world is out there, and now we have the key to open any door."

I close my eyes and smile. "English isn't just a language, Dad. It's a bridge to the world."

Dad whispers: "And we built that bridge together."

The **possibilities** are **endless**. And our story is just beginning.

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. "Remember when…?" — Lembra quando…?**
> Remember when I couldn't understand anything? / Remember when I was scared?

Estrutura para relembrar o passado com alguém:
- Remember when we started this course? (Lembra quando começamos esse curso?)
- Remember when you were afraid to speak? (Lembra quando você tinha medo de falar?)
- Remember when we couldn't order food in English? (Lembra quando não conseguíamos pedir comida em inglês?)

---

**2. "We can + verbo" — Conseguimos / Podemos + verbo**
> We can communicate. / We can always improve.

Estrutura que mostra capacidade e possibilidade:
- We can understand songs now. (Conseguimos entender músicas agora.)
- We can travel without fear. (Podemos viajar sem medo.)
- We can help other people. (Podemos ajudar outras pessoas.)

---

**3. "It's not… it's…" — Não é… é…**
> Learning English is not a destination — it's a journey.

Estrutura para redefinir algo com uma nova perspectiva:
- It's not the end — it's the beginning. (Não é o fim — é o começo.)
- It's not just a language — it's a bridge. (Não é só um idioma — é uma ponte.)
- It's not about being perfect — it's about communicating. (Não é sobre ser perfeito — é sobre se comunicar.)

---

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- A ideia de que "you don't need to be fluent to communicate" (você não precisa ser fluente para se comunicar) é muito valorizada em países de língua inglesa. Nativos respeitam e apreciam qualquer esforço de estrangeiros para falar inglês.
- Nos EUA e na Inglaterra, é muito comum pais e filhos aprenderem coisas juntos. Essa parceria de aprendizado fortalece o vínculo familiar e cria memórias especiais.
- A expressão "bridge to the world" (ponte para o mundo) reflete a realidade: o inglês é falado como primeira ou segunda língua por mais de 1,5 bilhão de pessoas no mundo. É a língua que conecta culturas diferentes.
- O conceito de "lifelong learning" (aprendizado ao longo da vida) é muito forte na cultura anglófona. Aprender nunca termina — cada dia é uma nova oportunidade de crescer.
