🎯Objetivo da Semana

Fazer amigos internacionais usando inglês e refletir sobre toda a jornada de aprendizado — celebrando conquistas e olhando para o futuro.

Nível: B2 — PARABÉNS! Esta é a ÚLTIMA aula do curso! Você chegou até aqui e agora vai celebrar suas conquistas e olhar para o futuro com confiança!

📖Textos da Semana

1️⃣ Making Friends Everywhere

Tema: Fazendo amizade com estrangeiro na viagem — se apresentar, trocar contatos, manter contato

Vocabulário: introduce yourself, where are you from, how long, recommend, local, tourist, exchange contacts, social media, keep in touch, hang out, invite, join us, pleasure meeting you, stay in touch, farewell

Duração: ~4-5 minutos de áudio

2️⃣ What English Gave Us

Tema: Reflexão final — pai e filho celebram o progresso e olham para o futuro com gratidão

Vocabulário: journey, progress, confidence, proud, grateful, opportunity, connect, communicate, improve, fluent, goal, achievement, milestone, beginning, endless

Duração: ~4-5 minutos de áudio

🧠Como Estudar os Textos

Passo 1: Compreensão Inicial

1. Leia o texto em português 3 vezes
2. Entenda a história/contexto completamente
3. Não se preocupe com inglês ainda

Passo 2: Listening com Texto

1. Escute o áudio junto com o texto em inglês (5 minutos)
2. Acompanhe cada palavra
3. Deixe o som entrar nos ouvidos

Passo 3: Listening com Tradução

1. Escute o áudio junto com o texto em português (5 minutos)
2. Conecte som com significado
3. Entenda o ritmo da fala

Passo 4: Listening Puro

1. Escute só o áudio em inglês (5 minutos)
2. Sem texto, sem tradução
3. Deixe o cérebro trabalhar

Passo 5: Anki (Memorização)

1. Adicione frase por frase no Anki
2. Cada frase com áudio
3. Revise diariamente

Passo 6: Shadowing (Fala)

1. Para cada frase do Anki, repita junto com o áudio
2. Tente 5 vezes por frase
3. Foco em pronúncia e ritmo

💡Dicas Importantes

✅ Faça todos os dias - Consistência é mais importante que quantidade

✅ Não se preocupe com perfeição - O objetivo é se acostumar com o som

✅ Repita muito - Seu cérebro precisa de repetição para aprender

✅ Use fones de ouvido - Melhor qualidade de som = melhor aprendizado

✅ Fale em voz alta - Não apenas escute, repita também!

✅ 15 min de listening por dia - Assista a playlist todos os dias!

❓Dúvidas Comuns

P: Preciso entender 100% do texto?

R: Não! O objetivo é se acostumar com o som. Entendimento vem com repetição.

P: Quanto tempo leva para aprender?

R: Cada pessoa é diferente. Alguns veem progresso em 2-3 semanas, outros em 1 mês. Seja paciente!

P: Posso pular o Anki?

R: Não recomendo. O Anki garante que você não esqueça o vocabulário. É essencial para dominar todas as frases que aprender nos textos!

P: Preciso fazer shadowing perfeito?

R: Não! O objetivo é treinar pronúncia e ritmo, não ser perfeito. Faça o seu melhor!

P: E se eu não entender nada da playlist?

R: Tá tudo bem! O objetivo é exposição. Seu cérebro está absorvendo mesmo sem você perceber. Continue assistindo e com o tempo vai entender cada vez mais!

US English

It's our third day in Miami, and the sun is shining. I'm at the hotel pool with Mom and Dad.

I jump in the water and start swimming. A boy about my age is playing with a ball nearby.

He throws the ball and it lands near me. I grab it and throw it back.

He smiles and says: "Thanks! I'm Oliver. Where are you from?"

I say: "I'm Lucas, from Brazil! Where are you from?"

"I'm from London, England. We're here on holiday too!"

I ask: "How long are you staying?"

Oliver says: "One more week. What about you?"

"We leave on Saturday. That's four more days!"

We start playing together in the pool. Oliver is really fun!

His mom walks over to my mom and says: "Hi! I'm Sarah. Your son and my son seem to be getting along well!"

Mom smiles and says: "I'm Carla. Pleasure meeting you! Are you enjoying Miami?"

Sarah says: "Oh, we love it! Do you have any recommendations? We're looking for good local restaurants."

Mom says: "There's a great seafood place two blocks from here. I totally recommend it!"

Sarah says: "That sounds wonderful! Would you like to join us for dinner tonight?"

Mom looks at Dad. Dad smiles and says: "We'd love to! That sounds great."

Oliver and I high-five. "Yes! Dinner together!"

At dinner, our families talk and laugh like old friends. Oliver's dad, James, tells us about tourist spots we should visit.

"You should go to the Everglades," he says. "It's amazing!"

Dad says: "Thanks for the tip! We'll definitely go."

After dinner, Oliver asks me: "Do you have Instagram? Let's exchange contacts so we can keep in touch!"

I say: "I don't have Instagram yet, but my mom does! And I have my dad's WhatsApp."

Our parents exchange phone numbers and social media. Sarah says: "Let's stay in touch! Maybe we can visit Brazil someday."

Mom says: "You're always welcome! It was a pleasure meeting your family."

Oliver and I shake hands and he says: "See you online, Lucas! Maybe we can play games together."

I smile and say: "Definitely! It was great hanging out with you!"

Walking back to the hotel, I feel happy. I made a friend from another country — and it all started with a ball in the pool.

Dad puts his hand on my shoulder and says: "See? When you try, making friends everywhere is easy."

US English

It's Sunday evening. The trip is over, and we're back home.

I'm sitting on the couch with Dad. The house is quiet. We're both tired but happy.

Dad looks at me and says: "Lucas, can you believe how far we've come on this journey?"

I smile and say: "Dad, remember when I couldn't understand anything in English? Not even a simple song!"

He laughs and says: "And remember when I was scared to talk to the hotel receptionist? I used to practice the sentence ten times before saying it!"

I say: "Now I can understand my favorite songs. I know what they're really saying!"

Dad says: "And now I can travel with confidence. I can ask for help, order food, and even make complaints when something goes wrong."

I think for a moment and say: "I made a friend from England, Dad. A real friend! We talk online every week now."

Dad smiles: "That's your achievement, Lucas. You did that by yourself. I'm so proud of you."

I feel warm inside. "I'm proud of you too, Dad. Remember when that tourist was lost downtown and you helped him with directions? His face was so happy!"

Dad says: "That was a great moment. I felt useful. English gave me that — the ability to connect with people from anywhere."

I say: "We're not fluent yet, right?"

Dad shakes his head: "No, not yet. But that's okay. We can communicate. We can understand and be understood. That's what matters."

I nod and say: "And we can always improve. Every day, a little more."

Dad says: "Exactly. Learning English is not a destination — it's a journey. And we've already reached so many milestones."

I count on my fingers: "We can introduce ourselves, order food, ask for directions, travel, make friends, solve problems…"

Dad adds: "Read articles, watch movies without subtitles sometimes, help tourists, and even think in English!"

I laugh: "That's true! Sometimes I think in English without noticing!"

Dad puts his arm around me and says: "You know what I'm most grateful for? That we did this together. Father and son, learning side by side."

I lean on his shoulder and say: "Me too, Dad. This was the best opportunity we ever gave ourselves."

Dad says: "And this isn't the end. It's just the beginning. The world is out there, and now we have the key to open any door."

I close my eyes and smile. "English isn't just a language, Dad. It's a bridge to the world."

Dad whispers: "And we built that bridge together."

The possibilities are endless. And our story is just beginning.

Eu fecho os olhos e sorrio. "Inglês não é só um idioma, pai. É uma ponte para o mundo."

Papai sussurra: "E nós construímos essa ponte juntos."

As possibilidades são infinitas. E a nossa história está apenas começando.
