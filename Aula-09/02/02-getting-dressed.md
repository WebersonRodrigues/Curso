# Texto 2 – Getting Dressed

## 📖 Texto – "Getting Dressed"

It is Monday morning. Time for school!

My mom **opens** my bedroom door and **says**: "Wake up! You need to **get dressed**."

I **get out** of bed and **open** my closet. I have many clothes inside.

First, I need to **choose** what to wear. I **look** at the window. It is cold and cloudy today.

My mom **says**: "It's cold outside. **Put on** something warm."

I **pick** a long-sleeve **shirt**. It is blue with white stripes.  
Then I **grab** my favorite **jeans**. They are dark blue and very comfortable.

I **put on** my **socks**. One is white and one is... also white. Good. They match!

I **sit** on the bed and **put on** my **sneakers**. I **tie** the laces slowly. Left over right, make a loop, pull it through. Done!

My mom **comes** in and **looks** at me. "You look nice! But you need a **jacket**. It's really cold today."

She **gives** me my red **jacket**. It is warm and has a zipper. I **zip** it up.

"Don't forget your **hat**!" she **says**.

I **put on** my gray **hat**. Now I am ready!

I **look** at myself in the mirror. Blue shirt, dark jeans, red jacket, gray hat, white sneakers. I look cool!

My little sister **comes** out of her room. She is wearing a pink **dress** with flowers, white **tights**, and her purple **boots**.

"I like your dress!" I **say**.

She **smiles** and **says**: "I like your hat!"

My mom **wears** a long black **coat** and a **scarf** around her neck. She also **carries** her **umbrella** because it might rain later.

Before we leave, my mom **checks** us:

"Jacket?" — "Yes!"  
"Hat?" — "Yes!"  
"Backpack?" — "Yes!"  
"Lunchbox?" — "Yes!"

"Okay, let's go!"

We **walk** to school together. The wind is cold, but I feel warm in my jacket.

At school, I **take off** my jacket and **hang** it on the hook. I **put** my hat in my backpack.

My friend Pedro **arrives** wearing a green **hoodie** and black **shorts**.

"Aren't you cold?" I **ask**.

He **laughs** and **says**: "No! I never feel cold!"

Boys are funny.

After school, it **starts** to rain. My mom **opens** her umbrella and we **walk** home together.

I **take off** my wet sneakers at the door and **put on** my **slippers**. So comfortable!

I **change** into my pajamas and **feel** warm and cozy.

I like choosing my clothes every morning. It is like a small adventure before the big adventure of the day!

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. "Put on" / "Take off" — vestir / tirar**
> I put on my jacket. / I take off my shoes.

Phrasal verbs essenciais para roupas:
- **put on** = vestir / colocar (roupa, sapato, chapéu)
- **take off** = tirar (roupa, sapato, chapéu)
- Put on your coat! (Vista seu casaco!)
- Take off your shoes at the door. (Tire os sapatos na porta.)

---

**2. "You need to + verbo" — você precisa + verbo**
> You need to get dressed.

Estrutura para expressar necessidade:
- You need to wear a jacket. (Você precisa usar uma jaqueta.)
- You need to tie your shoes. (Você precisa amarrar seus sapatos.)
- I need to change my clothes. (Preciso trocar de roupa.)

---

**3. "You look + adjetivo" — você está + adjetivo (aparência)**
> You look nice! / I look cool!

Usado para comentar sobre aparência:
- You look beautiful! (Você está linda!)
- You look tired. (Você parece cansado.)
- That looks great on you! (Isso fica ótimo em você!)

---

**4. "It's cold/hot outside" — está frio/quente lá fora**
> It's cold outside. Put on something warm.

Estrutura para falar do clima e roupas:
- It's hot today. Wear shorts. (Está quente hoje. Use bermuda.)
- It's raining. Take your umbrella. (Está chovendo. Leve seu guarda-chuva.)

---

**5. "Don't forget your…" — não esqueça seu/sua…**
> Don't forget your hat!

Imperativo negativo para lembretes:
- Don't forget your backpack! (Não esqueça sua mochila!)
- Don't forget your lunchbox! (Não esqueça sua lancheira!)
- Don't forget your umbrella! (Não esqueça seu guarda-chuva!)

---

**6. "Aren't you + adjetivo?" — você não está…?**
> Aren't you cold?

Pergunta negativa para expressar surpresa:
- Aren't you tired? (Você não está cansado?)
- Aren't you hungry? (Você não está com fome?)

---

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- Nos EUA, crianças geralmente **escolhem suas próprias roupas** para ir à escola (não usam uniforme na maioria das escolas públicas).
- **"Sneakers"** é o termo mais comum nos EUA para tênis. No UK, dizem "trainers".
- O **hoodie** (moletom com capuz) é uma das peças mais populares entre crianças e adolescentes americanos.
- Em dias frios, é muito comum mães americanas fazerem o "checklist" antes de sair: "Jacket? Hat? Gloves?" (Jaqueta? Gorro? Luvas?)
- **"You look nice!"** é um elogio muito comum e educado em inglês. Crianças aprendem desde cedo a elogiar os outros.
