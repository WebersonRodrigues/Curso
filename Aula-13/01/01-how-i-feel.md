# Texto 1 – How I Feel

## 📖 Texto – "How I Feel"

Today is field trip day! I wake up and I feel **excited**. I love field trips!

I eat breakfast fast. My heart is beating fast too. I am so **happy**!

My mom says: "Are you okay?" I say: "Yes! I am just excited!"

I get on the school bus. There are many kids. I sit next to my friend Lucas.

But then I feel a little **nervous**. I think: "What if I don't like the activities?"

Lucas says: "Don't worry! It will be fun!" I feel better now.

We arrive at the nature park. The teacher says: "Today we will climb the big wall!"

I look at the wall. It is very tall! I feel **scared** for a moment.

But I take a deep breath. I want to be **brave**. I say: "I can do this!"

I start to climb. My hands hold the rocks. I go up, up, up!

I get to the top! I am **surprised** — I did it! I feel so **proud** of myself!

My friends clap for me. I feel **happy** again.

At lunch, I sit with my friends. We talk and laugh. I don't feel **shy** today.

After lunch, we do a group activity. I am **worried** at first because I don't know some kids.

But they are nice! We work together. I feel **calm** and comfortable.

One boy wins a prize. I feel a little **jealous**, but then I think: "That's okay. I did my best."

On the bus home, I am tired but not **sad**. I had a great day!

I feel **grateful** for my friends and my teacher. They make everything better.

At home, I tell my mom about my day. She says: "I am proud of you!"

I don't feel **lonely** today. I feel loved.

Some days I feel **angry** about small things. But today is not one of those days.

Today I feel brave, proud, and grateful. It was a perfect day!

### 🔤 Vocabulário essencial do texto

| Inglês | Português | Dica |
|---|---|---|
| happy | feliz | "I feel happy" = me sinto feliz |
| sad | triste | oposto de happy |
| angry | com raiva | "I feel angry" = estou com raiva |
| scared | com medo | "I feel scared" = estou com medo |
| excited | animado | quando algo legal vai acontecer |
| nervous | nervoso | antes de algo novo |
| surprised | surpreso | quando algo inesperado acontece |
| proud | orgulhoso | quando faz algo bem |
| shy | tímido | quando tem vergonha de falar |
| worried | preocupado | quando pensa em coisas ruins |
| calm | calmo | tranquilo, relaxado |
| brave | corajoso | quando enfrenta o medo |
| grateful | grato | quando agradece por algo |
| lonely | sozinho/solitário | quando se sente só |
| jealous | com ciúmes | quando quer o que outro tem |

---

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. "I feel + adjetivo" — Eu me sinto + adjetivo**
> I feel excited. / I feel nervous. / I feel proud.

Usamos para dizer como estamos nos sentindo:
- I feel happy today. (Me sinto feliz hoje.)
- I feel scared. (Estou com medo.)
- I feel calm now. (Me sinto calmo agora.)

---

**2. "What if + frase?" — E se + frase?**
> What if I don't like the activities?

Usamos para expressar preocupação sobre algo que pode acontecer:
- What if it rains? (E se chover?)
- What if I can't do it? (E se eu não conseguir?)

---

**3. "I want to be + adjetivo" — Eu quero ser + adjetivo**
> I want to be brave.

Usamos para falar sobre como queremos ser:
- I want to be brave. (Eu quero ser corajoso.)
- I want to be calm. (Eu quero ser calmo.)
- I want to be happy. (Eu quero ser feliz.)

---

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- Nos EUA, **field trips** (passeios escolares) são muito comuns. As escolas levam os alunos a museus, parques e fazendas durante o ano.
- Falar sobre sentimentos é muito valorizado nas escolas americanas. Crianças aprendem a dizer "I feel..." desde pequenas.
- Muitas escolas nos EUA têm programas de **social-emotional learning** (aprendizado socioemocional) onde as crianças praticam identificar e expressar emoções.
- A expressão "I am proud of you" (estou orgulhoso de você) é muito usada por pais e professores americanos para encorajar as crianças.
