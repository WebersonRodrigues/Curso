# Texto 2 – Would You Mind?

## 📖 Texto – "Would You Mind?"

Today was a day full of situations where I had to be extra polite. Let me tell you about it.

It started at school. I didn't finish my homework because my computer crashed last night. I walked up to my teacher and said: "**I was wondering if** you could give me one more day to finish my essay. My computer stopped working."

She smiled. "**Of course!** Just bring it tomorrow, okay?"

"Thank you so much! **I really appreciate it.**"

At lunch, I realized I forgot my water bottle at home. I turned to the girl sitting next to me and asked: "**Would you mind** if I borrowed your extra water bottle? I forgot mine today."

"**Not at all!** Here you go," she said, handing it to me.

"Thanks! **I'd be happy to** return it after lunch."

After school, I was walking home when I got lost. I saw a woman at the bus stop and asked: "**Excuse me, could I possibly** ask you for directions? **Is it alright if** I bother you for a second?"

"**Go ahead!** Where are you trying to go?"

"I need to get to Maple Street. **Do you mind if** I look at your phone map?"

"**Feel free!** Here, let me show you. It's just two blocks that way."

"Thank you so much! You're very kind."

When I got to my street, I saw my elderly neighbor Mrs. Santos trying to carry heavy grocery bags. I ran over and said: "**Would you mind if** I helped you with those bags?"

She looked relieved. "Oh! **By all means!** That would be wonderful, dear."

"**No problem at all!** I'm happy to help."

I carried the bags to her door. She said: "**Would it be okay if** I gave you some cookies as a thank you?"

"**Sure thing!** I love cookies!"

She laughed. "You're such a polite young man. Your parents raised you well!"

At home, my mom was cooking dinner. I asked: "**Is it alright if** I use the computer now? I need to finish my essay."

"**Go ahead,** honey. Dinner will be ready in an hour."

I sat down and thought about my day. Being polite isn't hard — it just means thinking about other people's feelings. And you know what? People are always nicer to you when you're polite to them. It works both ways!

Depois da aula, eu estava andando pra casa quando me perdi. Vi uma mulher no ponto de ônibus e perguntei: "**Com licença, será que eu poderia** te pedir uma informação? **Tudo bem se** eu te incomodar por um segundo?"

Quando cheguei na minha rua, vi minha vizinha idosa, Dona Santos, tentando carregar sacolas pesadas de compras. Corri até ela e disse: "**A senhora se importaria se** eu ajudasse com essas sacolas?"

"**Sem problema nenhum!** Fico feliz em ajudar."

"**Com certeza!** Adoro biscoitos!"

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. "Would you mind if…?" vs "Do you mind if…?"**

> Would you mind if I helped you?  
> Do you mind if I look at your phone?

Ambas pedem permissão, mas com diferença de formalidade:

| Expressão | Nível | Quando usar |
|---|---|---|
| Do you mind if I…? | Educado | Com amigos, colegas, conhecidos |
| Would you mind if I…? | Muito educado | Com estranhos, professores, idosos |

Resposta positiva (= pode fazer): "Not at all!" / "Go ahead!" / "Of course not!"  
Cuidado: "Yes, I mind" = NÃO pode fazer (mas quase ninguém diz isso).

---

**2. "I was wondering if…" — forma super educada de pedir**

> I was wondering if you could give me one more day.

Usa o passado contínuo para soar mais gentil e menos direto:
- I was wondering if you could help me. (Eu queria saber se você poderia me ajudar.)
- I was wondering if I could leave early. (Eu queria saber se eu poderia sair mais cedo.)
- I was wondering if you had a pen. (Eu queria saber se você tem uma caneta.)

Mais educado que: "Can you help me?" (direto) ou "Could you help me?" (educado).

---

**3. "I'd be happy to…" — oferecer ajuda educadamente**

> I'd be happy to return it after lunch.

"I'd" = "I would" — forma educada de se oferecer:
- I'd be happy to help you with that. (Fico feliz em te ajudar com isso.)
- I'd be happy to explain again. (Fico feliz em explicar de novo.)
- I'd be happy to wait. (Fico feliz em esperar.)

Alternativas: "I'd love to!" / "Sure thing!" / "No problem!"

---

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- Nos EUA, ser **polite** (educado) é extremamente valorizado. Crianças aprendem desde cedo a dizer "please," "thank you," "excuse me," e a usar formas indiretas de pedir. Ser direto demais é visto como rude, mesmo entre amigos.
- **"Would you mind?"** é uma das expressões mais importantes do inglês educado. Americanos usam isso o tempo todo — com estranhos, professores, vizinhos, e até com amigos em situações mais formais.
- Nos EUA, é muito comum vizinhos se ajudarem, especialmente com idosos. Crianças que ajudam vizinhos são muito elogiadas. A frase "Your parents raised you well!" é um elogio enorme.
- **"Go ahead"** é uma resposta casual e amigável que significa "pode fazer" ou "pode falar." Americanos usam isso dezenas de vezes por dia. É mais natural que dizer "Yes, you can."
