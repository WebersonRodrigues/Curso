# Texto 2 – The Science Fair

## 📖 Texto – "The Science Fair"

Last month, our teacher announced that the school was going to have a **science fair**, and every student needed to create a project.

I was excited because I love science, but I was also nervous because a **judge** would evaluate our work.

First, I needed to choose a topic for my **experiment**. I decided to study how sunlight affects plant growth.

My **hypothesis** was: "I believe that the plant with sunlight will grow faster because plants need light to produce energy."

The reason I chose this topic is because I wanted to **prove** something that I had learned in class.

My teacher said: "A good **experiment** always starts with a clear **hypothesis** and a solid **method**."

So I planned my **method** carefully. I took two identical small plants and put them in the same type of soil.

I placed one plant near the window where it would get sunlight every day, and the other one inside a dark closet.

Every day for two weeks, I would **observe** both plants and **measure** their height with a ruler.

I used a notebook to **record** all the **data** — the height, the color of the leaves, and any changes I noticed.

During my **research**, I also read articles about photosynthesis to understand the science behind my experiment.

After one week, the **results** started to become clear. The plant with sunlight was already taller and greener.

The plant in the dark closet was growing very slowly, and its leaves were turning yellow.

I **recorded** everything carefully because I knew the **judge** would ask me about my **data**.

After two weeks, my **experiment** was complete. The plant with sunlight had grown twelve centimeters, but the plant without light had grown only four centimeters.

My **conclusion** was: "The **results** showed that sunlight is essential for healthy plant growth."

I wrote: "My **hypothesis** was correct because the plant with sunlight grew three times faster than the plant without light."

Then I prepared my **presentation** for the science fair. I made a poster with photos, graphs, and my **data**.

On the day of the fair, I set up my project in the school **laboratory** and waited for the **judges**.

When the **judge** came to my table, she asked: "Can you explain your **method** to me?"

I said: "I **observed** two plants for two weeks. I **measured** them every day and **recorded** the **data** in my notebook."

She then asked: "What did you learn from this **experiment**?"

I answered: "I learned that plants need sunlight to grow properly, even though both plants received the same amount of water."

The **judge** smiled and said: "Excellent **research**! Your **conclusion** is well supported by your **data**."

At the end of the fair, they announced the **awards**. I could not believe it when they called my name!

I won second place! I was so happy. **If I had** more time, **I would have** tested more plants with different amounts of light.

My teacher said: "I am proud of you. You followed the scientific **method** perfectly."

I learned that science is not just about getting the right answer — it is about asking good questions and **proving** your ideas with **evidence**.

### 🧠 Estruturas gramaticais do texto

**1. Hipótese com argumento — "I believe that... because..." (Eu acredito que... porque...)**
> I believe that the plant with sunlight will grow faster because plants need light to produce energy.

Usamos essa estrutura para apresentar uma ideia (hipótese) e dar uma razão lógica para ela:
- I believe that exercise is important because it keeps your body healthy. (Acredito que exercício é importante porque mantém seu corpo saudável.)
- I believe that reading helps you learn because it gives you new vocabulary. (Acredito que ler ajuda a aprender porque te dá vocabulário novo.)
- I believe that sleeping well is essential because your brain needs rest. (Acredito que dormir bem é essencial porque seu cérebro precisa descansar.)

---

**2. Causa e efeito — "The results showed that..." (Os resultados mostraram que...)**
> The results showed that sunlight is essential for healthy plant growth.

Usamos essa estrutura para apresentar o que os dados ou evidências provaram:
- The results showed that students learn better with music. (Os resultados mostraram que alunos aprendem melhor com música.)
- The data proved that the second method was faster. (Os dados provaram que o segundo método era mais rápido.)
- My experiment showed that temperature affects plant growth. (Meu experimento mostrou que a temperatura afeta o crescimento das plantas.)

---

**3. Third Conditional — "If I had..., I would have..." (Se eu tivesse..., eu teria...)**
> If I had more time, I would have tested more plants with different amounts of light.

Usamos "If I had + particípio, I would have + particípio" para falar de situações no passado que NÃO aconteceram — imaginando um resultado diferente:
- If I had studied more, I would have gotten a better grade. (Se eu tivesse estudado mais, teria tirado uma nota melhor.)
- If I had woken up earlier, I would have finished the project. (Se eu tivesse acordado mais cedo, teria terminado o projeto.)
- If she had brought her notebook, she would have recorded the data. (Se ela tivesse trazido o caderno, teria registrado os dados.)

### 🌍 Contexto cultural

- "Science fairs" (feiras de ciências) são muito populares nas escolas dos EUA, Canadá e Reino Unido. Os alunos criam projetos individuais, fazem experimentos reais e apresentam seus resultados para juízes. É uma tradição educacional que incentiva o pensamento científico desde cedo.
- O "scientific method" (método científico) ensinado nas escolas anglófonas segue passos claros: pergunta → hipótese → experimento → observação → dados → conclusão. Os alunos aprendem a usar esse método desde o ensino fundamental.
- Nos EUA, grandes feiras de ciências como a "Intel ISEF" (International Science and Engineering Fair) reúnem estudantes do mundo inteiro. Alguns projetos de alunos do ensino médio já resultaram em descobertas científicas reais!
- A palavra "data" em inglês é tecnicamente plural (o singular é "datum"), mas no dia a dia muitas pessoas usam "data" como singular: "The data shows..." em vez de "The data show..." Ambas as formas são aceitas no inglês moderno.
